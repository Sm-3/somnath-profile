About Me

Who I Am

I am a researcher specializing in computational approaches to understand biological systems. My work integrates deep learning architectures with molecular biology to predict protein interactions and functional annotations.

Currently developing PNILM (Protein Network Interaction Language Model), a novel framework that combines relational graph convolutional networks with ESM-2 protein language models for protein interaction prediction.

I am passionate about open science, reproducible research, and building tools that bridge the gap between raw biological data and actionable insight.
